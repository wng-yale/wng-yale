## Overview

This chapter covers long-term corporate financing mechanisms, specifically addressing how companies raise capital through equity and debt instruments.

## Long-Term Corporate Financing Options

The materials outline two primary financing approaches: "Selling Ownership: Stocks & Warrants" and "Borrowing: Bonds & Preferred Shares."

## Life Cycle of Stock

The document traces equity's journey from private ownership through public markets. Shares transition through design phases (voting rights, par value), initial public offerings via investment banking, and exchange trading (NYSE, AMEX, NASDAQ). The text notes that "a specialist makes a market in the shares, matching bidders and skers, i.e. buyers and sellers" on NYSE, while NASDAQ employs electronic matching systems.

## Bond Lifecycle

Bond issuance involves designing specific features including borrowing amounts, repayment schedules, financing constraints, callability, and sinking-fund provisions. Bonds are categorized as debentures (unsecured) or secured instruments, issued through syndicates and subsequently traded.

## Preferred Shares

These are characterized as non-voting shares with senior claims over equity holders regarding cash distribution, commonly held by corporations for tax advantages.

## Warrants

Defined simply as "Right to purchase shares for a fixed price, over a fixed period of time."
