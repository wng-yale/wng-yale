## Chapter Summaries Overview

The page provides condensed review notes for eight chapters covering fundamental investment concepts:

**Chapter I** addresses "geometric vs. arithmetic returns," historical capital market data, an "equity premium=8.5%," and standard deviation methodology.

**Chapter II** explores "risk and return," correlation principles, efficient frontier construction, and incorporating riskless assets.

**Chapter III** introduces "iso-utility curves," the "shortfall approach and Sharpe ratio," and tangency portfolio selection.

**Chapter IV** covers tangency portfolio theory, CAPM equilibrium, and a "'Birthday Cake' Proof" demonstration.

**Chapter V** explains how "correlations drive attractiveness of stock," introduces the security market line, and discusses beta relationships.

**Chapter VI** presents APT theory, arbitrage mechanics, and multi-factor systematic risk.

**Chapter VII** addresses beta estimation through regression, leverage effects, and discount rate applications in valuations and M&A.

**Chapter VIII** examines market efficiency, information processing, and three forms of market efficiency: weak, semi-strong, and strong.
