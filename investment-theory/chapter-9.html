Chapter IX

Financial Management: Long-Term Corporate Financing

Stocks, bonds, warrants, preferred shares, and the lifecycle of securities from creation to maturity.

Two Primary Mechanisms

Corporations finance themselves through two fundamental channels:

  • Selling Ownership: Stocks and warrants
  • Borrowing: Bonds and preferred shares

The Stock Lifecycle

Equity shares follow a journey from private creation through public trading:

  1. Initial design with voting rights and share structure
  2. IPO process through investment banking
  3. Trading on exchanges (NYSE, AMEX, NASDAQ)
  4. Market-making mechanisms ensure liquidity
  5. Portfolio holdings, dividend payments, and voting rights
  6. Potential acquisition, restructuring, or bankruptcy

The Bond Lifecycle

Bond issuance involves designing features including borrowing amounts, repayment schedules, financing constraints, callability, and sinking-fund provisions. Bonds may be secured (backed by specific assets) or unsecured debentures, issued via syndicates and held in portfolios until called, repurchased, defaulted, or matured.

Preferred Shares

Preferred shares occupy a middle ground: they offer non-voting status with senior cash claims over equity holders. They typically pay a fixed dividend and have priority in liquidation, but lack the upside potential of common stock.

Warrants

Warrants grant rights to purchase shares at fixed prices within specified timeframes. They function as long-dated call options issued by the company itself, and are often bundled with bond offerings to sweeten the deal for investors.
