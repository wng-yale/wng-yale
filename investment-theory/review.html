Review

Summary Review Notes

Concise chapter-by-chapter review for exam preparation.

Chapter I — Capital Markets and Investment Performance

Geometric vs. arithmetic returns, historical capital market performance (1926–1995), the 8.5% equity premium, and standard deviation as the primary risk measure.

Chapter II — The Geography of the Efficient Frontier

Risk-return relationships, correlation effects on diversification, and the concept of the efficient frontier — both with and without riskless assets. The Capital Market Line.

Chapter III — Preferences and Investor Choice

Iso-utility curves, risk aversion, the Sharpe Ratio, Value at Risk, and the tangency portfolio selection approach via the shortfall criterion.

Chapter IV — The Portfolio Approach to Risk

The tangency portfolio's role in CAPM equilibrium, the Two Fund Separation Theorem, and the "Birthday Cake" proof that all investors hold the market portfolio.

Chapter V — Further Explorations of the CAPM

How correlations influence stock attractiveness, the Security Market Line, the CAPM equation, and the key insight that portfolio beta equals the weighted average of component betas.

Chapter VI — The Arbitrage Pricing Theory

Ross's multi-factor alternative to the CAPM, arbitrage in expectations, macro-economic risk factors, and portfolio customization by factor exposure.

Chapter VII — Where Do Betas Come From?

Beta estimation through regression analysis, the amplifying effect of leverage on equity betas, and applications in discounted cash flow and M&A valuation.

Chapter VIII — Information and Market Efficiency

Weak, semi-strong, and strong-form efficiency. Event studies, the speed of price adjustment, and the limits of technical analysis and active management.

Chapter IX — Long-Term Corporate Financing

The lifecycle of stocks, bonds, preferred shares, and warrants. IPOs, market-making, and the design of corporate securities.
