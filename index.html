About

I study financial markets and securities, investment strategies, investor behavior, and financial history. My research demonstrates how finance has made the growth of civilizations possible by enabling the transfer of value across time — from ancient Mesopotamian accounting to the rise of modern capital markets.

I am the Edwin J. Beinecke Professor of Finance and Management Studies at the Yale School of Management, Director of the International Center for Finance, and a Research Associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research. I have served as president of both the Western Finance Association and the European Finance Association.

An Introduction to Investment Theory

A hypertext textbook for first-year MBA and MPPM students. Eight chapters covering portfolio theory, asset pricing, and market efficiency.

I

Capital Markets and Investment Performance

Returns, risk measurement, the equity premium, and historical capital markets data from 1926–1995.
II

The Geography of the Efficient Frontier

Markowitz portfolio theory, diversification, correlation, and the Capital Market Line.
III

Preferences and Investor Choice

Iso-utility curves, the Sharpe Ratio, Value at Risk, and the shortfall criterion.
IV

The Portfolio Approach to Risk

The CAPM, the tangency portfolio, Two Fund Separation, and index fund investing.
V

Further Explorations of the CAPM

The Security Market Line, beta estimation, expectations vs. realizations, and the Fama-French critique.
VI

The Arbitrage Pricing Theory

Stephen Ross's APT, multi-factor models, and arbitrage in expectations.
VII

Where Do Betas Come From?

Leverage, asset vs. equity betas, project valuation, and M&A analysis.
VIII

Information and the Efficiency of Capital Markets

Weak, semi-strong, and strong-form efficiency. Event studies and the limits of technical analysis.
IX

Financial Management: Long-Term Corporate Financing

Stocks, bonds, warrants, preferred shares, IPOs, and the lifecycle of securities.
Σ

Summary Review Notes

Concise chapter-by-chapter review for exam preparation.
