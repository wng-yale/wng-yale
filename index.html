About

I study financial markets and securities, investment strategies, investor behavior, and financial history. My research demonstrates how finance has made the growth of civilizations possible by enabling the transfer of value across time — from ancient Mesopotamian accounting to the rise of modern capital markets.

I am the Edwin J. Beinecke Professor of Finance and Management Studies at the Yale School of Management, Director of the International Center for Finance, and a Research Associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research. I have served as president of both the Western Finance Association and the European Finance Association.
